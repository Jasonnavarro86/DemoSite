Becoming a member.

People often wonder why becoming a member matters and why there seems to be so much to go through just to become part of the church. Here's the deal, we take membership very seriously because we believe that commiting to a church body is a very important and crucial decision.

Do you have to be a member to be a Christian? No, but when members join, they commit to a spiritual family that provides encouragement and support. They are called to a biblical degree of responsibility, service and sacrifice to their brothers and sisters. Becoming a member also unleashes new possibilities of connection within ministries and many volunteer opportunities..

Bethel 101

Your giving helps us support 12 missionaries across the globe. We have missionaries in Africa, Europe, the Middle East, South America and even here domestically in the U.S. We are able to partner with them and proclaim the gospel all around the world.

Dogma Class

In this 1 week class we cover some theological principles and beliefs of Bethel Christian Church. This is our open hand theology, these are things not necessary for salvation but things that we at Bethel hold as a standard for higher living.

16 Fundamental

Bethel is part of the Assemblies of God, the largest pentecostal denomination in the world. As part of the AG we adhere and uphold to the 16 fundamental truths of the council. In this 1 week class we cover these 16 important beliefs.

Baptism

We baptize by immersion because it best symbolizes the reality to which baptism points—our death and resurrection in Christ.If you have not been baptized following salvation, we ask you to take this step before becoming a Member. To help prepare you, we offer a Baptism Class. The class covers the biblical significance of baptism.
